Current Position

Postdoctoral Researcher

2025 – Present | McGill University, Canada

My research aims to uncover the mechanisms governing target-site selection of the Tn6022 transposon, with the ultimate goal of engineering proteins capable of recognizing alternative DNA sequences. I investigate the DNA regions that determine binding specificity, characterize molecular interactions within the targeting complex, and resolve its structure using cryo-EM. This work will provide mechanistic insights into how Tn6022 achieves exceptionally high transposition efficiency and establish a foundation for designing programmable DNA-targeting tools.
